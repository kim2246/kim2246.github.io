About Me

I am a 3rd Year Ph.D. Candidate in the School of Industrial && Systems Engineering at University of Wisconsin-Madison. I received my M.S. in Industrial Engineering from Seoul National University and B.S. in Creative Technology Management from Yonsei University. I research human-automation interaction, employing a mixed-method approach to explore, predict, and model human-vehicle interaction, social cooperation among road users, and driver behavior in dynamic traffic contexts. I closely collaborated with Honda Research Institute and Toyota CSRC on prosocial automation and driver override prediction, while my earlier academic work encompassed human-robot interaction, usability testing, and interface design for humanoid robots and EV sound design.
