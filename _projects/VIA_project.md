## Overview
We investigated **which UX factors drive acceptance and emotions** when older adults interact with an **AI-based voice user interface (VUI)**. Using a **Wizard-of-Oz** setup, we captured acceptance (utilitarian/hedonic & social dimensions) and affective responses, then **reduced UX dimensions via Exploratory Factor Analysis (EFA)** to reveal a compact factor structure actionable for VUI design in elder care.

## Methods
- **Participants:** N=53 older adults (60–82 yrs; screening for first-time VUI users).  
- **Paradigm:** **Wizard-of-Oz** (experimenter triggers system responses behind a partition to simulate natural VUI).  
- **Measures:**  
  - **Acceptance (11 items)** — anxiety, attitude, intention to use, adaptability, enjoyment, ease of use, sociability, usefulness, social influence, social presence, trust (Likert).  
  - **Affective (20 items)** — PANAS-based positive/negative affects (Likert).  
- **Statistical Analysis:** **Exploratory Factor Analysis (EFA)** with eigenvalue > 1; component extraction & interpretation.

## Outcomes
- **Acceptance → 2 components (≈71% variance):**  
  - **Intimacy** (social presence, sociability, trust, social influence, usefulness)  
  - **User engagement** (ease of use, adaptability, anxiety, enjoyment, intention, attitude)  
- **Affective → 5 components (≈71% variance):**  
  - **Internal-negative**, **External-negative**, **External-positive**, **Internal-positive**, **Awareness** (attentive/determined)  
- **Design implications:** Elder VUIs should (1) foster **rapport & social presence** (intimacy), (2) minimize **anxiety** via **clear, easy** flows, and (3) **signal availability** to support awareness; emotional responses depend more on **interaction quality** than device appearance.

## Examples
- **Conversation flows** that reduce anxiety (confirmation/repeats, clear repair strategies).  
- **Social presence cues** (warm voice, personable phrasing) to build intimacy.  
- **Awareness signaling** (idle prompts, “I’m here if you need me”) for always-on companionship.
